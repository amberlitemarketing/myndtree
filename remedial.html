Remedial Therapy

Developing the mind through exercises specific to students’ disabilities

Roughly 10% of all children in India have learning disabilities: issues not in their work ethic or determination, but in certain areas of their minds, making it difficult for them to work at the pace of their peers. Some examples of common learning disabilities include:

  • Dyslexia, a learning disorder that involves difficulty reading due to problems identifying speech sounds and learning how they relate to letters and words.
  • Dyscalculia, disability that makes it difficult to learn number-related concepts, perform accurate math calculations, and reason and problem solve.
  • Dysgraphia, which impairs writing ability and fine motor skills. It interferes with spelling, legibility, and word spacing and sizing and expression.
  • ADHD, which causes inattention (not being able to keep focus), hyperactivity (excessive movement) and impulsivity (hasty acts that occur without thought).

Learning disabilities can be incredibly difficult to work around, especially when undiagnosed. But, while they may never go away, proper attention and tutoring can make them less of an obstacle.

As long as a student is willing to put in the work to improve their learning capabilities, adequate attention and proper tutoring from a trained professional will help the student grow over time. Jasmine Panchamia is certified in Remedial Therapy for Learning Disabilities and is currently pursuing a bachelor’s degree in Special Education. She creates an accepting learning environment for all students, including those who have had to drop out of regular schools because of their rapid pace.
