Counseling

Assistance and guidance in resolving personal, social, or psychological problems and difficulties.

A 2016 National Mental Health Survey found that 83% of Indians suffer from mental health issues but do not have the adequate resources to cope with them. Figuring out the signs of a mental illness on its own is difficult, especially when traits of disorders like anxiety and depression (excessive worrying, sadness or hopelessness) can appear to be regular emotions at first glance. However, one of the most difficult steps in the process is deciding to get help.

Whether or not a patient knows their diagnosis, or even has a diagnosis, having a professional to talk to is one of the most effective ways to handle the pressures of life and deal with complications of the mind.

Counseling can also be for external struggles as well as internal; a diagnosis or mental illness is not necessary to seek help. Counseling can be sought for anything, including, but not limited to, relationships, grief, self-esteem, anger, stress, or just needing someone to talk to.

Counseling sessions and their cost depend on the individual and problems specific to them, but all include a safe, non-judgemental environment with guaranteed confidentiality. Jasmine Panchamia has her masters in psychology and is certified in Behavior Therapy and Relaxation Training and Techniques; while patients are talking to a professional, they still feel comfortable and are able to express themselves honestly.
